The Supercontinent is the single landmass of Seises during its supercontinent era. It covers roughly 37% of the planet's surface and was assembled by the convergence of 22 tectonic plates, whose collisions raised its mountain belts and whose interior it now binds into one contiguous mass. Its highest summit reaches about 7,370 m above sea level.

This article is a stub. In later eras the Supercontinent will rift apart into separate continents, the stage on which the history of Seises unfolds.
