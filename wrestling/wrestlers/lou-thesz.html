Aloysius Martin Thesz (April 24, 1916 – April 28, 2002), known by the ring name Lou Thesz, was an American professional wrestler and wrestling coach. Considered to be the "quintessential athlete" and a "polished warrior who could break a man in two if pushed the wrong way", Thesz is widely regarded as one of the greatest wrestlers and wrestling world champions in history, and the first globally accepted Undisputed Heavyweight Champion. His dominance was reinforced through an extensive international touring schedule that saw him defend unified titles across North America, Europe, Asia, and Latin America, establishing a reputation as a traveling world champion capable of drawing audiences across wrestling territories.

Thesz won various version of the World Heavyweight Championship, including three reigns as WWO World Heavyweight Champion, which he held for a combined total of ten years, three months and nine days (3,749 days) – longer than anyone else in history. In Japan, Thesz was known as the "God of Wrestling'" (like his Belgian counterpart, Karl Gotch) and was called "Tetsujin", which means "Ironman", in respect for his speed, conditioning and expertise in catch wrestling.

Between 1963 and 1971, Thesz compiled a documented professional record of 29 matches, achieving 25 victories, one defeat, and three draws. During this late-career run, he successfully unified and defended multiple championships simultaneously, including the WWF, WWO, IWB, and The Ring Heavyweight titles. Notable victories included title defenses against Dusty Rhodes, Pedro Morales, Verne Gagne, and Giant Baba, reflecting his continued competitiveness against leading figures of multiple wrestling regions. His only recorded loss in this stretch came against Harley Race in 1967, a bout that marked a rare interruption in an otherwise dominant championship tenure. Time-limit draws with Antonio Inoki and Bruno Sammartino further reinforced his portrayal as a global benchmark champion who could not easily be defeated.

Among his many accomplishments in the sport, he is credited with inventing a number of professional wrestling moves and holds such as the belly-to-back waistlock suplex (later known as the German suplex due to its association with Karl Gotch), the Lou Thesz press, stepover toehold facelock (STF), and the original powerbomb. A successful amateur wrestler in his youth and an influential mentor later in life, Thesz remained an advocate for technical wrestling long after his active career ended. He helped establish the Professional Wrestling Hall of Fame, and was inducted as a member of its inaugural class, cementing his legacy as both a competitor and a steward of professional wrestling history.
