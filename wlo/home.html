The World Lucha Organization (WLO) is an international professional wrestling organization based in Mexico. Founded in 2024, the WLO aims to promote and elevate the sport of Lucha Libre on a global scale. With a rich heritage rooted in Mexican wrestling traditions, the WLO brings together the world's top wrestlers to compete in thrilling matches and championships. The organization is committed to preserving the unique style and culture of Lucha Libre while fostering a competitive and entertaining environment for fans worldwide.

The WLO hosts a weekly show on thursdays, which mostly consist in classical Relevos Australianos 3 vs. 3 tag team matches, as well as several Pay-Per-View events, including their flagship show Libremania that is held every second saturday of September to conmemorate the independence of Mexico. WLO stream their matches on their Youtube channel.

Championships

The WLO's prestigious blue championship belt proudly displays the flags of member countries of the organization. All WLO world title belts share the same distinguished design, regardless of weight class. The organization recognizes four primary divisions—Welterweight, Super Middleweight, Middleweight, and Heavyweight—along with Middleweight and Criuserweight for women’s wrestling. You can see the championship reigns here.

The WLO holds monthly pay-per-view events. The former four Openweight Grand Prixs have been replaced by the yearly Open Tournament. Spots in the Open are guaranteed for reigning champions. The remaining spots in the bracket are filled by public vote, in a manner similar to an all-star game. A reigning champion may choose to forfeit participation in the Open Tournament, but doing so forfeits any rematch privileges and may only be done once. If the champion still holds the title the following year and enters the tournament, the restriction is reset.

In June, instead of a standard pay-per-view, the WLO features Extreme Fights, such as the Sol de Sangre, MurderBrawl or Homenaje a WCW: FallBrawl War Games event. These events often include brutal formats like War Games, Gauntlet, Ladder, and other extreme matches. Homenaje a WCW features a 4 vs 4 War Games match. The losing team must face each other in randomly assigned pairs at the next pay-per-view in a Relevos Increíbles 2 vs. 2 tag team match. Those who lose in the Relevos Increíbles must then face mask vs mask, mask vs hair, or hair vs hair Lucha de Apuestas matches at Libremania.

WLO Rules

In WLO, all matches are contested under a one-fall format, with victory achieved via pinfall or submission. Disqualification occurs if a competitor uses weapons, removes an opponent’s mask, or commits other severe infractions. While open-palm strikes are permitted, closed-fist punches are strictly forbidden. The sport operates without a VAR review system, meaning referees' decisions are final, and match results cannot be overturned. However, post-match reviews may lead to fines or disciplinary actions if violations are discovered after the fact.

Rankings and recognition

WLO has an official ranking for each weight class, which determines a competitor's ability to challenge for a title. These can be overhauled with the Grand Prix tournaments, events, and/or at the promoter's discretion. WLO also recognizes WWE as the only other promotion with valid world championships, following WWE's introduction in April 2026 of four men's weight divisions and two women's weight divisions, equivalent to WLO's own structure. WWE also maintains a tag team division open to both men and women.

All unified champions are required to defend both their WLO and WWE titles at WLO pay-per-view events. However, only the WWE titles are defended at WWE shows. This distinction exists because lucha libre, while a form of professional wrestling, adheres to stricter rules prohibiting closed fists and the removal of masks. Wrestling as a broader category permits these actions, and it would be fundamentally unfair to demand that lucha libre titles be defended under conditions that violate their core principles.
