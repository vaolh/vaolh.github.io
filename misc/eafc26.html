Amine Samir Boukhari (born 3 October 2003) is a French professional footballer who plays as a midfielder for Premier League club Arsenal and the France national team. Widely regarded as one of the best players in world football, Boukhari is known for his exceptional goal-scoring ability from midfield, creative vision, technical skill, and playmaking prowess.

Born in Marseille to Algerian parents, Boukhari began his professional career with Stade Lavallois in Ligue 2, where his prolific performances quickly attracted attention from Europe's elite clubs. After establishing himself as one of France's most promising talents, he secured a €45 million move to Real Betis in 2029, where he enjoyed unprecedented success. During his three seasons in Seville, Boukhari won three consecutive La Liga titles and three Pichichi Trophies, becoming the club's all-time leading scorer with 165 goals across all competitions. His remarkable 2031-32 season saw him register 30 assists in La Liga, setting a new record for assists in Europe's top five leagues.

In 2032, Boukhari transferred to Arsenal in a record-breaking deal, where he immediately guided the Gunners to an unprecedented quadruple in his debut season—winning the Premier League, FA Cup, EFL Cup, and UEFA Champions League. His extraordinary first campaign in England saw him score 42 league goals and provide 24 assists, winning both the Premier League Golden Boot and Playmaker of the Season awards. He also netted 17 Champions League goals, equaling Cristiano Ronaldo's single-season record.

At international level, Boukhari made his senior debut for France in 2029 and has since earned 31 caps, scoring 30 goals. He was instrumental in France's run to the 2030 FIFA World Cup final, where they finished as runners-up, and helped Les Bleus claim victory at UEFA Euro 2032. With four European Golden Shoe awards by age 30, Boukhari is considered one of the most complete and prolific attacking midfielders of his generation.
