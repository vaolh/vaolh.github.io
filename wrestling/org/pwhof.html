International Wrestling Board

The International Wrestling Board (こくさいレスリングひょうぎかい,Kokusai Resuringu Hyōgikai) (IWB) is an international professional wrestling organization based in Japan. It is among the three major organizations which sanction professional wrestling bouts, alongside the World Wrestling Federation (WWF) and the World Wrestling Organization (WWO). The IWB sanctions championships across four men’s divisions: Welterweight, Middleweight, Bridgerweight, and Heavyweight; and two women’s divisions: Lightweight and Featherweight. Its prestigious belts trace their design lineage to the “Big Green Belt” version of the WWF World Heavyweight Championship (1983–1984), which was introduced during Hulk Hogan’s Japan tour and later used as the basis for the original Triple Crown title. When the IWB established its own championship system, the design was adapted into six new titles with red straps, now collectively referred to as the “Big Red Belt.” Governance is carried out by a General Assembly of officials, promoters, trainers, and wrestlers, which oversees rules, rankings, mandatory challengers, and championship recognition.
