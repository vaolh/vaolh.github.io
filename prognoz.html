Colby the Computer, who has the entire Bible programmed into his memory.

This very very cute stuffed anthropomorphic computer is called Colby. This particular version (bis) is a rendition by hyperreal artist Prognoz Pogodi, using an array of mysterious AI tools including Kaiber Superstudio. There is an incredibly comprehensive overview of Pogodi's previous social media activity and artistic endeavours by Shel Raphen. I will be referring to this text extensively so I recommend a short skim before continuing. The objective of this article is to first provide a review and (mostly positive) critique on Prognoz Pogodi's more recent work. The second concerns the future of art in a world of hyperreality and simulacra. One of the first things you can learn from said article is that the author was fascinated by the anthropomorphic dollar Fiveish, whose origins lie as a mascot of the Jewish program Fiveish and Friends. As you can see from the image, this motif has continued, but is there anything connecting these characters other than their peculiar appearance? It turns out, there is. The common denominator, or mantra, is that both Gerbert (another character prominently featured) and Colby are also mascots of religious programs. Both come from Catholic productions Gerbert and Colby's Clubhouse respectively. Speak on religious themes a little.
