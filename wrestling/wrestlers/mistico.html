Luis Ignacio Urive Alvirde (born December 22, 1982), better known by his ring name Místico (Spanish for "Mystic"),is a Mexican luchador enmascarado (or masked professional wrestler). Throughout his career, Urive has primarily wrestler as a técnico (scientific wrestler), with brief periods of time where he showed signs of a more ambiguous affiliations and straddled the técnico/rudo divide. Místico has been the biggest box office draw in Mexico for a number of years.

Urive was trained for his professional wrestling debut by his father Dr. Karonte, his uncle Tony Salazar, and Fray Tormenta, a wrestling priest who also acted as his Padrino ("Godfather") early on. Místico will often pose with his hands put together in a "praying" motion as well as adorning his tights with crucifix like patterns. Early in his career he wore mainly white trunks and masks with either silver or gold adornment, earning him the nickname "El Príncipe de Plata y Oro" ("The Prince of Silver and Gold"). With the years passing and his legend growing, Mistico is now known as "El Rey de Plata y Oro" (The King of Silver and Gold).

Místico defeated Bandido to win the World Wrestling Organization (WWO) and The Ring welterweight championship in 2019.

One of lucha libre's biggest ever stars, Místico is known as an excellent high flyer, able to execute complex dives both in and out of the ring. The Ring magazine has noted that his La Mística submission finishing move is often executed so fast that his opponents do not have time to react until it is locked in.
